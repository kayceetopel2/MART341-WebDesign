How-To Break the Cycle of ADHD Paralysis

8 Strategies to Get Back In Motion from the ADD Association

  1. The Daily Brain Dump. Write down your thoughts, and review which ones are a priority.
  2. Create your tasks to be achievable. Breaking projects up into smaller projects creates easy wins
    to help you feel like you've accomplished something.
  3. Keep your work schedule simple. Instead of trying to plan your work schedule for the entire day,
    try to designate time for one task
  4. Focus on completion, instead of perfection. Allowing yourself to focus on completing a task
    will have you crossing off more things on your list compared to when you are trying to make everything perfect.
  5. Make room for rewards. After completing a task find something small to reward yourself to keep
    the motivation going to complete another one.
  6. Get up and move around. Inbetween large projects or routine tasks take the time to move around to prevent burnout.
  7. Try to keep things interesting. Repetitive work is challenging, so try to find ways to make things interesting
  8. Do things you love. It is important to still make time for things that you enjoy daily to decrease
    mental exhaustion and reduce stress.
